The Bullet is a 1970 Dodge Charger — long, heavy, and riding a long wheelbase at around
1,795 kg. That layout gives it the best high-speed stability of the muscle group and the
worst agility: it is slow to rotate, and once a slide starts it builds momentum and takes
distance to gather. You steer this one with the throttle and plan your slides early.

## How it drives

On tarmac the Bullet is rock-steady in a straight line and through fast corners, but it is
reluctant to change direction. The long wheelbase and front weight mean it takes a set
slowly, so you set the car up early, get it pointed, and drive out on the torque with mild
power oversteer. Slow, tight corners are where it struggles most — it simply does not want
to rotate at low speed.

On loose surfaces its slides are long and lazy. Once the rear steps out it carries a long
way before it hooks back up, so smooth, early inputs beat quick flicks — commit to the
slide and ride it rather than trying to snap it back.

In a pack the Bullet is a tank. Its length and mass make it hard to shift, and its
high-speed stability lets it hold a line through contact that would unsettle lighter cars.
