The Stahlwagen is a rear-drive estate modelled on a 2001 Mercedes C-Class Estate (W203), and it is
the stable, predictable member of the balanced-RWD group. Its long wheelbase and wagon mass, which
sits slightly rearward at around 53/47, help the rear axle put power down, and a torque-rich
midrange means it drives cleanly out of corners. Where the lighter sports cars snap, this one flows
— its oversteer is controllable rather than a threat.

## How it drives

On tarmac the Stahlwagen turns in with confidence and settles into corners without the nervous edge
of a short-wheelbase car. The slightly rearward balance and long wheelbase give it good traction
and a stable platform, so exit oversteer, when it comes, arrives progressively and is easy to catch
rather than snapping away from you. It rewards a tidy, committed line more than constant correction.

On loose surfaces the same stability holds up: it slides in a controllable, predictable way that you
can steer with the throttle, and the torque-rich midrange means there is real drive to lean on out
of the corner. Because the car is so predictable, brake-bias and differential tuning make more of a
difference here than the raw fight-the-balance changes a nose-heavy car needs.

In a pack the wagon mass and long wheelbase make it stable through contact and hard to unsettle. It
is not a fragile momentum toy — it holds its line and can lean on its size when the racing gets
rough.
