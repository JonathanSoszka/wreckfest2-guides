The Switchback is a late-'60s Chevrolet Chevelle-style muscle car, a front-biased V8
brawler at roughly 55/45. Its strength is speed in a straight line; its weakness is tight
corners, where the nose weight makes it push wide. It is happiest on fast, flowing tracks
and ovals and least happy threading a slow, technical section.

## How it drives

On tarmac the Switchback is a straight-line weapon that fights you when the road tightens.
Fast sweepers are fine, but in tight corners the heavy front understeers and it is slow to
change direction, so you help turn-in every way you can and brake early into the technical
bits rather than carrying speed in. Once it is pointed, the V8 torque drives it out
cleanly, with the usual muscle-car power oversteer if you stab the throttle.

On loose surfaces the same story holds — it slides wide on entry and needs to be set and
settled before you get back on power. Manage the understeer with your braking and your
line, not by trying to flick it.

In a pack it is a stable, heavy presence that holds its line at speed, so use the fast
sections to make places and defend carefully through the slow ones where it is weakest.
