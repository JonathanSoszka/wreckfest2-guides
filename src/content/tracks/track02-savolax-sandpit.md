Savolax is a quarry track, and it drives like one: long routes cut through sand banks and
slopes, three-quarters or more gravel, with only short tarmac links tying the loose sections
together. It is the venue where loose-surface technique matters most — get it right and you
gain time everywhere, get it wrong and you are ploughing through sand.

## Driving the loose stuff

Gravel rewards patience and a car that rotates. You brake earlier than instinct says, get the
turn done while the car is slowing, and feed the power back in progressively as the corner
opens. Stabbing the throttle mid-corner just lights up the wheels and washes you wide into the
sand banks that line most of the track. The banks are not just scenery — you can lean on them
in places, but hit one square and it stops you dead.

Because the surface is deep and consistent, this is where a gravel-biased setup pays off:
softer, more compliant suspension to keep the tyres in the dirt over the ruts, and a
differential set up for traction out of the slow stuff. The gravel columns in the tuning
guides are dialled for exactly this kind of track.

## Main versus Short, and reverse

The venue ships four ways to run it: the long Main Route and a tighter Short Route, each with
a reverse. The Short Route is even more gravel-dominant (82%) and strings the slow corners
closer together, so it punishes a setup that only works when the car is settled. The reverses
change which banks you are braking toward — worth a few laps each to relearn, since a corner
you attacked downhill one way you now climb into.

## What to work on here

Savolax is the best place to practise gravel braking and throttle patience — the two skills
that separate quick loose-surface drivers from slow ones. See
[Braking on Loose Surfaces](/lessons/braking-on-loose-surfaces/), and lean on the gravel tab
of whichever [tuning guide](/tuning/) fits your car.
