This is a short stadium-style rallycross track: a tight loop that mixes tarmac with dirt
sections and repeats quickly, so the racing is close and the surface under your tyres is
always changing. It runs a main layout and a reverse.

## Short, busy, and physical

Rallycross circuits are deliberately compact — corners come fast, there is little room to
recover a mistake, and the field stays bunched. That makes clean lines and consistent
braking matter more than outright top speed, and it makes contact almost constant. Expect to
be leaned on into every slow corner.

The dirt sections are shorter than at a full gravel track like Savolax, but they still catch
people out precisely because they are brief — you carry tarmac confidence onto a loose patch
and understeer off. Anticipate each transition and lift a fraction earlier than feels
necessary.

## What to work on here

The tight, repetitive layout makes this a good place to work on racecraft in traffic:
holding your line under contact and picking the one corner per lap where an overtake is
actually on. See [Racing With Contact](/lessons/racing-with-contact/) and
[Braking on Loose Surfaces](/lessons/braking-on-loose-surfaces/). A balanced hybrid setup
suits the constant surface swaps better than a single-surface tune.
