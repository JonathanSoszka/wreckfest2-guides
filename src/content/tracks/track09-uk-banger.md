UK Banger is the British oval-racing venue: a dirt track run as ovals and a figure-eight,
with the rough-and-tumble spirit of banger racing where contact is part of the format rather
than a foul. It ships three layouts around the same infrastructure.

## Short-oval banger racing

Like the paved speedway, a banger oval is about momentum and pack management more than
braking — but on dirt, so the car moves around underneath you and the line drifts wider as
the surface cuts up over a race. Hold a tidy line, protect your momentum through the two
main corners, and accept that you will be leaned on constantly.

The figure-eight layout adds a crossover where traffic meets head-on. On a loose surface that
crossing is even twitchier than on tarmac, because a car arriving sideways has less control
over exactly where it ends up. Time it with margin — commit early or lift early, never dither
in the middle.

## What to work on here

Banger ovals are pack-racing and contact practice on a loose surface: momentum management,
holding position when you get hit, and reading the figure-eight crossings. See
[Racing With Contact](/lessons/racing-with-contact/) and
[Braking on Loose Surfaces](/lessons/braking-on-loose-surfaces/). A stable dirt setup that
shrugs off contact serves you better than a nervous quick one.
