In most racing games contact is a mistake. In Wreckfest it is a tool, and cars are built to
take it. Learning when to lean on someone, when to absorb a hit, and when to just leave is
most of what separates a clean top-three from a spin into the barrier.

## Giving contact that helps you

The useful hit is a nudge to the corner of a car, not a battering-ram to the middle. Catch
a rival's rear quarter as they turn in and they rotate wide, opening the inside for you. Hit
them square in the door and you both scrub speed and the pack eats you. Aim for the corners,
time it to their turn-in, and use the least contact that does the job.

## Taking contact without losing the car

When you know a hit is coming, get the car straight and settled before it lands. A car that
is already sliding or mid-rotation will spin from a tap; a car that is square and stable
will shrug off a much bigger hit. If you are about to be leaned on through a corner, give up
the tight line early, brace the car straight, and take the position back on exit where you
have traction and they do not.

## When to just leave

The race is long and the barriers are unforgiving. If a fight is going to put you into a
wall or cost you three seconds of recovery, concede the corner and settle in behind. In a
game where everyone is trading paint, the driver who stays pointed the right way while
others spin picks up places for free.

## Damage awareness

Contact has a cost beyond the moment — accumulated damage dulls the car. Trading paint to
hold a position is fine; grinding down the whole field for one place early in a long race
leaves you a wounded car for the end of it. Spend your car's health where it changes the
result.
