<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Valken is a nose-heavy front-driver with modest power that handles well — a momentum car. You
win by carrying corner speed, not by out-dragging anyone, so the whole setup is tuned for agility.
**Soft front, firm rear** loosens the tail and points the nose so you can keep the car turning
without lifting to a crawl.

The **differential** governs the driven front wheels. It carries a little extra lock on loose to
hook up the launch — this car will light the fronts off the line — and opens on grip so it stops
fighting your steering. Keep the coast side low so the driven axle does not push on entry, and gear
it for corner exit since there is not much power to stretch a tall final drive.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud plus low power means the driven front bogs and pushes. Soften the front, raise the ride height,
and **lock the diff up** so both front wheels drag the car out together.

Feed the throttle in progressively off the line and out of corners — this car spins the fronts
easily, and wheelspin in mud just digs you in and kills the momentum you rely on.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel rewards the Valken's momentum and sits close to the hybrid setup. Lift-off rotation points
the nose — back off to rotate, then feed the power in and carry speed out.

Keep the rear bar firm so the nose stays willing, and mind the launch: a clean, progressive getaway
on gravel is worth more than any straight-line tweak on a car this light on power.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

Asphalt exposes exit understeer as the loaded front runs out of grip. Stiffen the rear, drop the
car, firm the front to use the grip, and **open the diff** so it stops fighting your steering out of
the corner.

Add front negative camber to plant the outside edge, and stay smooth on the throttle off the line —
even on grip this car will spin the fronts if you mash it, and the launch is where you gain or lose
places.

</Fragment>
