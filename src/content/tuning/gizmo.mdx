<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Gizmo is tiny and light with a short wheelbase, so it darts into corners and rotates readily —
sometimes too readily, since it carries pronounced lift-off oversteer on loose surfaces. That
rotation is a tool, but it can bite, so the rear is stiff without going overboard. **Soft front,
firm rear** points the nose, and you lean on the light chassis to carry corner speed rather than
power.

The **differential** governs the driven front wheels: locked on loose to put the weak power down,
opener on grip so it stops fighting your steering. Keep the coast side low — the light rear already
comes round on the lift, so you do not need much braking lock to rotate it. Gear it short and keep
the little engine in its torque out of slow corners.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud punishes the weak power-down worst — the driven front wheels dig and the nose pushes. Soften
the front, raise the ride height, and **lock the diff up** so both front wheels claw the car out
together.

The light rear will still rotate on the lift, so let the entry do the turning and roll smoothly onto
the power; you cannot bully your way out of mud with this little engine.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel suits the Gizmo — it sits close to the hybrid setup and the lift-off rotation becomes a
genuine weapon. Back off to bring the nose round, then feed the power in and carry the momentum out.

Keep corrections tiny; on a pad the short wheelbase means a big stick input overshoots fast, and
this car is already eager to rotate.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

Asphalt's grip exposes exit understeer as the driven front runs out of grip. Stiffen the rear, drop
the car, firm the front, and **open the diff** so it stops fighting your steering out of the corner.

Add front negative camber to plant the outside edge, but do not chase more rear stiffness — the
light tail already rotates on lift, and overdoing it makes the car snappy on grip.

</Fragment>
