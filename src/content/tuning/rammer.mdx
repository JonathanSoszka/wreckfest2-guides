<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Rammer is a heavy understeer tank, so the whole setup fights the nose to find bite. The
**anti-roll bars** are the biggest lever: a soft front bar and a stiff rear bar shift roll stiffness
rearward, which frees the front to turn in and lets the tail rotate the car for you. Plenty of front
camber and a soft front spring back that up.

The **Differential** runs opposite to instinct: locked on loose, opener on grip. On mud and gravel a
tight diff drives both rear wheels and puts the torque down; on asphalt it would snap both wheels
loose together, so opening it keeps the exit progressive. Brake Pressure's scale runs 50–150%, so
100% is mid-travel, not the maximum — and this car sits above it, because a 1,633 kg sedan needs a
lot of stopping.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud is the lowest-grip surface and the Rammer's weight works against it — the nose pushes wide and
the rear digs in. Soften everything, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and **lock the diff
up** so both rear wheels drive instead of spinning the inside one away. Back the brakes off; a heavy
car locks its wheels easily on a loose surface.

Brake early and in a straight line, then let the car coast-rotate into the corner — trying to
trail-brake this much mass into mud just plows the front.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel sits close to the hybrid setup — the changes are small. The soft-front, stiff-rear bar split
does its best work here, letting the tank rotate on a surface that still forgives a slide. Steer it
with the throttle and keep corrections small, which matters on a pad where a big stick input
overshoots.

Use the car's momentum. Roll the speed through the corner rather than braking hard and trying to
point it — a heavy sedan carries far more than it accelerates.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

On asphalt the Rammer finally has grip to lean on, so the springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure
climbs, and the car drops closer to the road. The **diff opens up**: on high grip a locked diff
breaks both rear wheels loose together, so a looser diff keeps the exit progressive. More front
camber helps the outside tyre find an edge through the reluctant nose.

Brake early and smooth — this is a momentum rammer, not a late-braker. Set the entry speed before
you turn and let the mass carry you out.

</Fragment>
