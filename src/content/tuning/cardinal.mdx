<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Cardinal is the heaviest car in the roster, with a high top speed, huge strength, and the worst
cornering of anything on the grid. You do not tune it into a corner-carver; you gear it long, lean
on its mass and durability, and claw back whatever front grip you can. A soft front bar against the
**stiffest rear bar** in these guides is the main lever, backed by plenty of front camber and a very
firm front spring to cope with the nose weight.

The **Differential** runs locked on loose, opener on grip: a tight diff drives both rear wheels on
mud and gravel, while opening it on asphalt stops both wheels breaking loose together. The **Final
Drive** runs long — this car wants top speed, not acceleration, and its mass carries the speed it
builds. Brake Pressure's scale runs 50–150%, so 100% is mid-travel; a 1,937 kg car sits well above
it because stopping this much mass takes everything.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud punishes weight, and the Cardinal has the most of it — the nose plows and the rear sinks. Soften
everything, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and **lock the diff up** so both rear wheels
drive instead of spinning the inside one away. Ease the brakes back; all that mass locks the wheels
easily on a loose surface.

Brake early, in a straight line, and let the car settle before you ask it to turn. Trail-braking
two tonnes into mud just buries the front.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel sits close to the hybrid setup — small changes. The soft-front, stiff-rear bar split does its
best work on a surface that still lets a heavy car rotate, so use it: steer with the throttle and
keep corrections small, which matters on a pad where a big stick input overshoots.

Carry the momentum. The Cardinal takes an age to rebuild speed, so roll it through the corner rather
than scrubbing it all off and trying to point the nose.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

On asphalt the Cardinal has grip to lean on at last, so the springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure
climbs to its highest, and the car drops closer to the road. The **diff opens up**: on high grip a
locked diff breaks both rear wheels loose together, so a looser diff keeps the exit progressive.
Maximum front camber helps the outside tyre find an edge through the heavy understeer.

Use the straights — this is where the long gearing and high top speed pay off. Brake early and
smooth into the corners, set your entry speed before turning, and let the mass carry you through.

</Fragment>
