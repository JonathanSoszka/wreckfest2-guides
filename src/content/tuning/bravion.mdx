<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Bravion already rotates — a near 50/50 saloon with a rev-happy engine that will oversteer on
power and on lift. So the job here is the opposite of a muscle car: calm the rear rather than wake
it. The **Differential** runs less power lock than a nose-heavy car needs (it doesn't need help
rotating), with a helping of coast lock to settle the lift-off snap into corners.

The rest is a steadying hand. Brake balance sits forward to stop the entry snap, the rear anti-roll
bar stays soft for exit traction, and rear toe-in keeps the tail planted. Follow the surface for
everything else: locked diff and soft/high on loose, opener diff and stiff/low on grip. Brake
Pressure's scale runs 50–150%, so 100% is mid-travel, not the maximum.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud has the least grip, so the eager rear is easiest to catch out here. Soften the springs and
bars, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and **lock the diff up**: on low grip a tight diff
drives both rear wheels instead of spinning the inside one away, and the loose surface still lets
you steer with the throttle.

Feed the power in smoothly and let the nose lead — stabbing the throttle just lights the rear up
and throws away the Bravion's balance.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel is the middle ground and sits closest to the hybrid setup — the changes are small. The
Bravion slides willingly and predictably here; steer it with the throttle and keep corrections
small, which matters on a pad where a big stick input overshoots.

Let it rotate a touch on power and drive out, but don't chase the slide — this car rewards a tidy
line over a showy one.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

On asphalt the Bravion has grip to lean on, but the rev-happy rear will still snap if you get
greedy. The springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure climbs, and the car drops closer to the road —
and the **diff opens up**: on high grip a locked diff breaks both rear wheels loose together, so a
looser diff keeps the exit progressive.

Roll onto the throttle in one smooth motion out of the corner. This is a point-and-shoot exit car
when you're patient with the right foot; jab it and the tail steps out.

</Fragment>
