<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Switchback is a heavy straight-line brawler with a reluctant nose — it wants to understeer in
the tight stuff. The setup is built to free that nose: a soft front **anti-roll bar** and a stiffer
rear bar shift grip forward and let the tail come round, and extra front camber gives the outside
tyre something to bite with on turn-in.

The **Differential** is still the biggest lever, and it runs opposite to instinct: locked on loose,
opener on grip. Brake early — a heavy car takes distance to slow, and a slight rear brake bias
helps it rotate as you trail into a corner. Brake Pressure's scale runs 50–150%, so 100% is
mid-travel, not the maximum.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud is the lowest-grip surface and the understeer only gets worse — the heavy nose pushes wide.
Soften the springs and raise the ride height to clear the ruts, keep the rear bar stiff relative to
the front to keep some rotation, and lock the diff up to drive both rear wheels.

Brake in a straight line before you turn — a heavy car that is still slowing mid-corner just plows
straight on.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel sits close to the hybrid. The loose surface takes some of the edge off the understeer, so
the car rotates more willingly — steer it with the throttle and keep corrections small, which
matters on a pad where a big stick input overshoots.

Slow the car early, get it pointed, then drive out; trying to brake and turn together wastes the
front tyres.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

Asphalt is where the Switchback belongs — high speed and long corners suit its stability. The
springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure climbs, and the car drops closer to the road, but the
front bar stays soft relative to the rear to keep the nose alive. The **diff opens up** on grip so
the rear stays progressive on exit.

Brake early into the technical sections and let the long gearing carry you everywhere else — this
car makes its time on the fast parts, not the hairpins.

</Fragment>
