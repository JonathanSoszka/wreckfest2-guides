<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The **Differential** is the RoadSlayer's biggest lever, and it runs opposite to instinct: **locked
on loose, opener on grip.** On mud and gravel, low grip means an open diff just spins the inside
rear away — locking it drives both wheels and puts the torque down (the loose surface still lets
you rotate on the throttle). On asphalt, a locked diff snaps both rear wheels loose together, so
opening it up keeps the exit progressive.

Everything else follows the surface: soft/high/less-brake on loose, stiff/low/more-brake on grip.
The rear stays a touch softer than the front (springs and bar) so the torque hooks up on exit
rather than snapping.

Worth noting: the rear springs run much softer than the front, and brake Pressure's scale runs
50–150%, so 100% is mid-travel, not the max.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

The RoadSlayer's weight and low-down torque work against it in mud — the rear digs and lights up.
Soften the springs and bars, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and **lock the diff up**: on
low grip a tight diff drives both rear wheels instead of spinning the inside one away, and the
loose surface still lets you steer with the throttle.

Roll onto the power smoothly out of corners rather than stabbing it — a bogged-down muscle car is
very hard to recover.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel is the RoadSlayer at its most fun, and it sits close to the hybrid setup. It oversteers on
exit, which is what you want on a loose surface — steer it with the throttle and keep corrections
small, which matters on a pad where a big stick input overshoots.

Let the car rotate on power and drive out of it; do not try to hold it dead straight.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

On asphalt the RoadSlayer has real grip to lean on, but it snaps the rear on power if you get
greedy. The springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure climbs, and the car drops closer to the road
— and the **diff opens up**: on high grip a locked diff breaks both rear wheels loose together, so
a looser diff keeps the exit progressive.

Keep some compliance even here — Wreckfest's tarmac is bumpy and kerbed, and a mid-corner bump can
step the rear out unexpectedly.

</Fragment>
