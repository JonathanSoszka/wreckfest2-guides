<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Rocket is the rare muscle car with no single vice to fight, so these numbers sit close together
front to rear — the **anti-roll bars** and springs are nearly matched, which is what keeps it
neutral. Small moves swing it either way, so treat this hybrid as a centre point and lean it toward
turn-in or stability to taste.

The **Differential** is still the biggest lever, and it runs opposite to instinct: locked on loose,
opener on grip. On mud and gravel a tight diff drives both rear wheels and puts the torque down; on
asphalt it snaps both loose together, so opening it keeps the exit progressive. Brake Pressure's
scale runs 50–150%, so 100% is mid-travel, not the maximum.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud is the lowest-grip surface, and even a balanced car will light the rear up early. Soften the
springs and bars, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and lock the diff up so it drives both
rear wheels instead of spinning the inside one away.

Feed the throttle in smoothly out of the slow corners — the Rocket rotates willingly, so a stab of
power just wastes it in wheelspin.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel is where the Rocket's balance pays off — it sits close to the hybrid and asks for almost
nothing. The car slides predictably at both ends, so steer it with the throttle and keep
corrections small, which matters on a pad where a big stick input overshoots.

Let it rotate on power and drive out of the slide; do not try to hold it dead straight.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

On asphalt the Rocket has real grip to lean on. The springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure
climbs, and the car drops closer to the road — and the **diff opens up** so a locked rear axle
can't break both wheels loose together on exit.

Keep some compliance even here — Wreckfest's tarmac is bumpy and kerbed, and a mid-corner bump can
step the rear out on a car this neutral.

</Fragment>
