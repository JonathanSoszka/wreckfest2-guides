<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Crusader is the textbook front-driver: nose-heavy, modest torque, and prone to understeer the
moment you ask the front to steer and drive at once. The whole setup is about taking work off the
front axle. **Soft front, stiff rear** — a firm rear bar and springs loosen the tail so the nose
rotates on entry, then you protect the loaded front on the way out.

The **differential** governs the driven front wheels here. It locks up on loose surfaces to put the
modest torque down, and opens on grip so it stops fighting your steering — a tight driven diff on
tarmac just piles on understeer. Keep the coast side low so the driven axle does not push on entry,
and bias the brakes forward while leaving a slice at the rear to help the light tail come round.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud is where front-drive understeer bites hardest — the driven wheels dig and the nose washes wide.
Soften the front, raise the ride height to clear the ruts, and **lock the diff up** so both front
wheels pull the car out instead of spinning the inside one away.

Get all your rotation done on the entry lift and roll onto the power smoothly; stabbing the throttle
just loads the front and pushes you straight on.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel is the Crusader at its best and sits close to the hybrid setup. Lift-off rotation is your
friend here — ease off to bring the nose round, then feed the power back in and drive out straight.

Keep the rear bar firm so the tail stays willing to rotate, and keep corrections small; the modest
torque means momentum is everything on a loose surface.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

Asphalt exposes the exit understeer most clearly — the loaded front runs out of grip trying to do
two jobs. Stiffen the rear further, drop the car, firm the front to use the grip, and **open the
diff** so it stops fighting your steering out of the corner.

Add more front negative camber to keep the outside edge planted, and hold onto that slice of rear
brake so you do not lose the entry rotation the car leans on.

</Fragment>
