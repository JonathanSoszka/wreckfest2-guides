<Fragment slot="hybrid">

#### Why these numbers

The Bullet is long, heavy and long in the wheelbase — the best high-speed stability of any of the
muscle cars, and the worst agility. Its slides build slowly and take distance to gather, so the
setup buys rotation for the slow corners: a soft front **anti-roll bar** and a stiffer rear bar
free the nose, extra front camber wakes it on turn-in, and a long **Final Drive** trades launch for
top speed.

The **Differential** is still the biggest lever, and it runs opposite to instinct: locked on loose,
opener on grip. Once it is sliding, steer with the throttle and be patient — a car this long does
not snap back quickly. Brake Pressure's scale runs 50–150%, so 100% is mid-travel, not the maximum.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="mud">

#### Why these changes

Mud is the lowest-grip surface and the long car feels its worst here — reluctant to turn, slow to
gather a slide. Soften the springs and raise the ride height to clear the ruts, keep the rear bar
stiff relative to the front to buy rotation, and lock the diff up to drive both rear wheels.

Slow it well before the corner and give every input time to take — a heavy cruiser punishes rushing.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="gravel">

#### Why these changes

Gravel sits close to the hybrid, and the loose surface helps the long car rotate. Let the momentum
work for you — steer it with the throttle and keep corrections small, which matters on a pad where a
big stick input overshoots on a slide this slow to gather.

Point it early, commit, and drive out; do not try to hold it dead straight.

</Fragment>
<Fragment slot="asphalt">

#### Why these changes

Asphalt rewards the Bullet's stability at speed. The springs and bars stiffen, brake pressure
climbs, and the car drops closer to the road, but the front bar stays soft relative to the rear to
keep the reluctant nose turning. The **diff opens up** on grip so the long rear stays progressive on
exit.

Use the long gearing on the straights and brake early for the slow corners — the Bullet makes its
time carrying speed, not diving into hairpins.

</Fragment>
